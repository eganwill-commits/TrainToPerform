The Methodology

One methodology.
Dosed to meet you where you are.

Train To Perform is built on a principle most programs ignore: the framework is universal, but the dose has to change with the athlete. Teens building their athletic ceiling train differently than adults balancing performance with work and family. Same pillars, same order — different dosing.

MVTmobility → PWRpower / SKLskill → STRstrength → CONDconditioning → FINfinishing
What stays the same

Six pillars, same order, every session. Movement primes the body and nervous system. Power and skill come next, while the system is fresh. Strength follows. Conditioning builds the engine. Finishing reinforces the structure underneath. Quality first, volume last — break the order and you compromise what came before.

What changes with the athlete

The weighting. Teens prioritize power and skill during the window the nervous system learns fastest. Adults balance strength, conditioning, and mobility for performance under real-world load.

The engine underneath.

The pillars are what gets trained. Progressive overload is how it improves — each block adds load, intensity, or complexity in calibrated steps, one past where you were, inside what the body can absorb.

The Coach

Built from sport. Shaped by the hard parts.

Sport shaped how I think, move, and show up. Today my arena is functional fitness and competitive CrossFit — I've ranked in the top 1% worldwide in the CrossFit Open for my age group multiple times and placed highly in well-regarded global competitions.

My foundation runs through team sports first, then a deep love for CrossFit I owe in large part to TJ and Allison Belger of TJ's Gym — coaches who shaped how I think about training, community, and what a gym can be for an athlete.

From there I pursued a functional-fitness path through Marcus Filly and Functional Bodybuilding. When I faced my first major injury — a torn rotator cuff, labrum, and bicep requiring surgery — Marcus and his team helped me work through it. That taught me what I needed: custom, prescribed coaching to rebuild correctly, stay healthy for life, and still compete at an elite level. That lesson is now built into how I coach.

It all adds up to a formula for developing high-performing athletes, in sport and in life. That's why Train To Perform exists as a methodology, not just a program — built to make athletes better at every stage, because bodies, lives, and seasons change, and the training should change with them.

"Today I will do what others won't, so tomorrow I can accomplish what others can't." / Jerry Rice

Whether you're chasing a first pull-up or a first podium, or holding onto capacity through a demanding decade, the methodology meets you where you are. That's the whole point.
